Submission Guidelines

Academus Journal welcomes original research and analytical essays from high school students worldwide in the humanities and social sciences. We aim to maintain a rigorous, student-driven peer review process while encouraging emerging voices in academic discourse.

Eligibility

  • Authors must be currently enrolled in high school (grades 9–12 or equivalent).
  • Submissions must be the original work of the student. Collaborative work is allowed, but all contributors must meet the eligibility criteria.
  • Each student may submit up to one papers per issue.

Acceptable Topics

We accept papers in fields such as (but not limited to):

  • Philosophy, Ethics
  • Political Science, Law
  • History, Art History
  • Literature, Critical Theory
  • Sociology, Anthropology
  • Economics (qualitative)
  • Education, Media, Cultural Studies

Manuscript Requirements

  • Length: 1,500–4,500 words (not including bibliography or footnotes)
  • Format: Typed, 1.5 space, 12 pt font (Times New Roman), must be .pdf
  • Style: Chicago or MLA; use footnotes as needed and include a bibliography
  • Language: English only
  • Originality: Plagiarism will result in automatic rejection

What to Include in Your Submission

In a single document, include the following:

  1. Title Page: Title, Author Name, School
  2. Manuscript

Review Process

  • All submissions are double-blind peer reviewed by trained high school editors
  • Decisions within 4–6 weeks: Accept, Revise & Resubmit, or Reject

How to Submit

Submit using our Google Form, or email samcao920@gmail.com with subject line: Submission: [Your Paper Title].

Deadlines

  • Fall Issue: Submissions due by September 30
  • Spring Issue: Submissions due by March 31

Notes

  • No fees for submission or publication
  • Only previously unpublished work is accepted
